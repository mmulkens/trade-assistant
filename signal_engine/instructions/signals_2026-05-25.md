## What changed from v1 to v2

The v1 scan produced 51 signals. The v2 refinements brought this down to 4. The primary drivers of the reduction:

| Refinement | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Breakout freshness check** | Eliminates breakouts on day 2+ of an already-established move. Nearly all 36 v1 breakout signals were stale — the close had already exceeded the 50-day high on a prior day. |
| **EMA21 touch/recovery requirement** | Replaces the old "within 2% of EMA21" proximity check. Price must have actually touched or pierced the EMA (same-bar wick, or prior-day breach with today's recovery). Many v1 pullback signals fired on approach without contact. |
| **MACD histogram shape — prior decline required** | The histogram must have been getting more negative before it ticked up. Without this, the check fired on any single improving bar regardless of context. |
| **Guard A and B as general pre-filters** | EMA chain alignment and freefall rejection now gate both strategies, not just Strategy A. |

This is consistent with the fact that the EU equity market has been in a multi-week rally: most breakouts occurred earlier in the move, and the stocks are now extended rather than breaking fresh ground.

**No hard cap triggered:** All stops are within normal ATR range. This is a direct result of the freshness check eliminating stale breakouts — stale entries inflate stop distances because the structural swing low is fixed while entry price has risen.

**No dual-strategy signals:** As expected, pullback and breakout are nearly mutually exclusive at the same moment.

**Signal count of 4 is a credibility check, not a concern:** The v2 engine is more selective by design. Four genuine setups out of 298 tickers on a single day is a normal outcome — it means the engine is emitting only setups that satisfy all conditions simultaneously, not proximity approximations.
